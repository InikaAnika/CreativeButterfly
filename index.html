What does it mean to be creative?

Creativity is usually defined as the ability to solve problems in different ways. Being creative means being able to take risks. It means to be able to solve problems in different ways/ innovating new ways, and being willing to try new ways. Everyone has creativity inside them, it is just a matter of how much they are “capable”/ want to use their creativity.

Why be creative?

Why should you be creative? Being creative is a way to stretch your brain while having fun! Yes, you still need to do your homework and go to school, but it is good to exercise your brain in different ways. Being creative also allows you to solve real world problems, whether it is designing a golf course or trying to entertain yourself when you are bored.

"Boosting” creativity in Kids

You can “boost” your creativity in different ways. Here are some tips:

  1. If you have a project where you need to be creative, try to do it before breakfast, if you can’t, do not do the project right after eating.
  2. To be creative you need to be well rested. Keep in mind that I am a kid too, and I love to stay up late, but I am just stating the facts.
  3. Being stressed often affects creativity, so if you can’t get something done in time, ask a teacher for a little more time.
  4. Mindfulness activities can really calm the brain so try one of those if you have time. Mindfulness is not always meditating, it can be coloring in quiet while focusing or doing a craft/ origami that you know.
  5. It is important for kids to know creativity does not mean being perfect.
  6. Kids often stop trying because they think if they are not good at it the first time round they have failed.
  7. It is important to show examples to people of how you can become better if you practice. Yes creativity comes with practice too. If you make the same drawing three times you will end up making some new changes to it everytime. That is creativity.
  8. It is almost required to give kids more materials so that they can choose the material that sparks their imagination.
  9. It is also important to give lots of examples but let them choose what they want to make either through one of the examples or something based on their imagination. The examples might just prove to be an inspiration to their creativity.

My experiments

I tested out a few different experiments:

First Experiment - Painting while listening to different genres of music

The first experiment was testing what results you would get between 2 different genres of music (Classical [violin] or pop [Taylor Swift]). I did this by putting on classical music and then painting 4 paintings, then I put on the pop music and drew 4 more paintings the next day at approximately the same time of day. The one with Taylor Swift was 3 out of 4 paintings were abstract while the Classical was 2 out of 4 paintings were abstract. Conclusion for this experiment was that different genres of music can lead to different types of creativity. So find your music as it can definitely boost creativity.

Second Experiment - Remote Associates Test

The second experiment was testing whether you were more creative during different times of day. We tested this using puzzles called Remote Associates Test. These are puzzles where you give three words like fly, salted, cream you would then see which word fits into all of them. In this case butter, because it forms butterfly, salted butter, and buttercream.The result came out better before breakfast. I am not saying to skip your breakfast, but I think we are most creative early in the morning.

Third Experiment - Creativity and Age

The third experiment was to see how creativity varies in people of different age groups. I painted this image with an adult and a younger child. We all saw an image, and then we painted adding our creativity to it. Conclusion for this is that creativity has no one definition. Creativity in an adult might be different and for a child it might be completely different. Beauty is not creativity. Even though as we get older we might paint perfectly but notice how in the little child's version there is snowfall which is creativity in its own unique way. Perfection is not creativity.
